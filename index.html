THE BIOLOGICAL VAULT

COURSE: COMM2754 - Digital Media Specialisation 1

TIME: Vietnam Semester 1 2026

GROUP NAME: Vinfast

MEMBERS: Vo Dinh Duc An, Nguyen Tuan Kiet, Le Minh Nhat, Nguyen Tran Quang Huy


THEME: SDG 14 - Life Below Water

QUOTE: "We possess ocean wealth, and this precious asset brings benefits to us all. So why don't we know how to preserve it?" - Thu Minh

CONCEPT: Abstract DNA (Data Flow) & Corruption

AUTHOR: Vo Dinh Duc An

01. CONCEPTUAL FRAMEWORK

This generative piece abstracts the coral reef into a "Biological Database". Based on research identifying reefs not only as the "medicine cabinet of the 21st century" but also as a multi-billion dollar economic asset, the healthy reef is visualized as an organic, flowing stream of genetic data and value symbols.

02. THE DESTRUCTIVE TOUCH

The user's cursor acts not merely as physical interference, but as a "Data Corruptor". When touched, the harmonious streams violently glitch, turning into dead, grayscale binary codes. This delivers a profound conceptual impact: destroying a coral reef is erasing an irreplaceable archive of humanity's future.

03. SDG & QUOTE CONNECTION - CALL TO ACTION

This poignant question posed by Thu Minh strikes at the heart of the paradox addressed by SDG 14: Life Below Water. Humanity relies heavily on the ocean's "wealth"—an invaluable biological, pharmaceutical, and economic database stored within fragile ecosystems like coral reefs. Yet, despite being the primary beneficiaries of this natural asset, our collective actions continuously drive its degradation through pollution, climate change, and negligence.

In the Biological Vault generative artwork, this contradiction is simulated perfectly: the moment the user interacts with the harmonious DNA streams, they become a "Data Corruptor," violently glitching and erasing the very data that sustains life. To answer Thu Minh's challenge, we must bridge the gap between simply extracting the ocean's value and taking accountability for its survival. We must transition from passive consumers to active stewards of marine life. The call to action is urgent: we must acknowledge the fragility of this ecological codebase and halt our destructive interference. Don't touch coral reefs; protect the biological vault before this irreplaceable wealth is deleted forever.
