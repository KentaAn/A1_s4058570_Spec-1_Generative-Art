THE BIOLOGICAL VAULT

COURSE: COMM2754 - Digital Media Specialisation 1

TIME: Vietnam Semester 1 2026

GROUP NAME: Vinfast

MEMBERS: Vo Dinh Duc An, Nguyen Tuan Kiet, Le Minh Nhat, Nguyen Tran Quang Huy


THEME: SDG 14 - Life Below Water

QUOTE: "We possess ocean wealth, and this precious asset brings benefits to us all. So why don't we know how to preserve it?" - Thu Minh

CONCEPT: Abstract DNA (Data Flow) & Corruption

AUTHOR: Vo Dinh Duc An

01. CONCEPTUAL FRAMEWORK

This generative piece abstracts the coral reef into a "Biological Database". Based on research identifying reefs not only as the "medicine cabinet of the 21st century" but also as a multi-billion dollar economic asset, the healthy reef is visualized as an organic, flowing stream of genetic data and value symbols.

02. THE DESTRUCTIVE TOUCH

The user's cursor acts not merely as physical interference, but as a "Data Corruptor". When touched, the harmonious streams violently glitch, turning into dead, grayscale binary codes. This delivers a profound conceptual impact: destroying a coral reef is erasing an irreplaceable archive of humanity's future.

03. SDG & QUOTE CONNECTION - CALL TO ACTION

"Thu Minh’s statement, “We possess ocean wealth, and this precious asset brings benefits to us all. So why don't we know how to preserve it?” (Thu Minh, cited in Danh Anh, 2016), highlights the contradiction between humanity’s dependence on the ocean and our failure to protect it. This concern directly relates to Sustainable Development Goal 14 (Life Below Water), which aims to conserve and sustainably use marine resources for future generations. Despite the ocean’s ecological and economic importance, human activities such as tourism, pollution, and careless physical contact continue to damage fragile ecosystems, especially coral reefs. Corals are living organisms that can be easily harmed by even a simple touch, leading to stress, disease, and long-term reef degradation. Protecting ocean wealth therefore begins with small but responsible actions. One simple yet powerful step is clear: don’t touch coral reefs.
